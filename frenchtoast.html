Why I Love French Toast

When I was younger my mom used to make French Toast for breakfast. I took one bite and I instantly fell in love. It had a really soft and mellow texture while maintaining a crisp edge, since it was fried in butter. My mom's toast was stuffed with instant melt cheese, which added a nice savory aftertaste to it. I searched the internet for a comparable recipe and my research has yielded this.

Classic French Toast Recipe

Ingredients:

  • 4 slices of thick bread (brioche, challah, or sourdough work well)
  • 2 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup milk or cream
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1/2 tsp ground cinnamon
  • Pinch of salt
  • 2 tbsp butter for frying
  • Maple syrup, powdered sugar, and fresh fruit for serving

Instructions:

  • Prepare the Batter: In a shallow bowl, whisk together the eggs, milk or cream, vanilla extract, cinnamon, and salt until well combined.
  • Soak the Bread: Dip each slice of bread into the batter, allowing it to soak for about 20-30 seconds on each side. Ensure the bread is well-coated but not overly saturated.
  • Heat the Pan: Heat a large skillet or griddle over medium heat and add the butter. Once the butter is melted and bubbly, place the soaked bread slices onto the skillet.
  • Cook to Perfection: Cook the bread for 3-4 minutes on each side or until golden brown and crispy. Adjust the heat as needed to prevent burning.
  • Serve and Enjoy: Serve your French toast warm, topped with maple syrup, powdered sugar, and fresh fruit. Enjoy the delightful contrast of textures and flavors!

Variations to Try

While the classic French toast recipe is delicious on its own, there are a number of variations that you can try out. PS, I've included the links for the recipes.

Stuffed French Toast

Elevate your breakfast by stuffing the bread with cream cheese, Nutella, or fresh fruit before dipping it in the batter. This adds a delightful surprise with every bite.

Savory French Toast

For a savory twist, omit the vanilla and cinnamon from the batter and add a pinch of salt and pepper instead. Serve with bacon, cheese, or saut√©ed vegetables for a hearty meal.

French Toast Casserole

If you're feeding a crowd, a French toast casserole is a great option. Layer slices of bread in a baking dish, pour the batter over the top, and let it soak overnight. Bake in the morning for an easy and impressive breakfast.

Tips for the Perfect French Toast

  • Choose the Right Bread: Thick, sturdy bread works best for French toast. Day-old or slightly stale bread is ideal as it absorbs the batter without becoming too soggy.
  • Use Whole Ingredients: Whole milk or cream, fresh eggs, and pure vanilla extract will enhance the flavor and texture of your French toast.
  • Don't Rush the Soak: Allow the bread to soak in the batter for enough time to absorb the flavors, but not so long that it falls apart.
  • Cook at the Right Temperature: Medium heat is key to achieving a crispy exterior and a soft interior. Too high heat will burn the outside before the inside is cooked through.
